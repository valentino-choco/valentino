About Me

Hello! I'm Valentin, a 21-year-old French student pursuing a double degree between SupAero in France and the University of Michigan in the US. My passion lies in the observation and exploration of the Universe, which fuels my enthusiasm for both astronomy and mathematics. When I'm not delving into the mysteries of space, you can often find me scaling new heights through my love of climbing!

What I've done

  • November 2018

    Humanitarian trip to Morocco

    Atlas Mountains, Morocco

    During high school, I participated in a humanitarian trip to Morocco with the association MonPlaisir Solidarité. Accompanied by dentists and nurses, we visited remote villages in the Atlas Mountains, north of Ouarzazate. Our mission was to treat children's dental issues, educate them on oral hygiene, and distribute toothbrushes and toothpaste.

    It was an unforgettable experience: the excitement of never knowing where we would sleep each night, the joy of meeting the children in these remote villages, savoring Morocco's delicious couscous and pastries, and gazing at the stunning night sky!

  • May-June 2023

    Rocket Launching in Sweden with CNES!

    Esrange Space Center, Sweden

    As a first-year student at SupAero, I had the privilege of participating in a CNES-sponsored project known as Sera-4. The project's goal was to design and launch a small rocket, a collaborative effort involving students and engineers from CNES and ArianeGroup. Our successful rocket launch took place in early June 2023 at the Esrange Space Center in Sweden.

    I was there for one week, in the very north of Sweden, where the sun never sets at this time of the year as it is above the Polar Circle!

  • June-July 2023

    Internship at Pic du Midi Observatory

    Pic du Midi, Pyrenees, France

    At the end of my first year at SupAero, I completed a one-month internship at the Observatory Midi-Pyrenees, specifically at the Pic du Midi Observatory. I spent days living atop this mountain at 3000 m (9800 ft) of altitude, in a place isolated from the world!

    During the nights, I observed the skies using the Bernard Lyot Telescope, the largest telescope on French soil. During the days, I enjoyed the breathtaking mountain landscapes above the clouds and occasionally went near the coronagraph, a specialized telescope used for observing the sun.
